Say Hello to Our Staff

Leah Paley • LGSW

Executive Director

Leah has been an influential part of the LARS team since 2011, serving as Director of Homeless & Emergency Services and most recently as Deputy Director. During this time she has gained a real understanding of the needs of the Laurel community and a drive to ensure that LARS is meeting those needs. Having overseen the daily operation of LARS’ Crisis Center, Leah has the right perspective to bring LARS’ vision to life as the agency develops its strategic plan for the coming year. She has heard the stories and struggles of LARS’ clients, and these first-hand experiences remain at the heart of her leadership.

Leah brings significant experience to her role at LARS, including management of statewide advocacy efforts for the National Multiple Sclerosis Society. Leah is a graduate of the University of Maryland, Baltimore where she received her Master’s in Social Work (MSW) with a concentration in Management and Community Organizing and a specialization in Social Action and Community Development. She is a Licensed Graduate Social Worker (LGSW) in the state of Maryland.

Stephanie Guzmán • BSW

Director of Emergency Services & Community Relations

Stephanie has worked as a case manager since 2012 after she earned her Bachelor of Social Work degree from the University of Texas at Austin. Her work at a domestic violence shelter exposed her to the challenges facing underprivileged communities and ignited her passion for working with people to better their lives. Stephanie has carried that commitment and compassion for the under privileged and underserved into her work at LARS where she serves as Director of Emergency Services and Community Relations. She continues to be inspired every day by the resiliency of her clients and the compassion she witnesses from those who support LARS.

Lauren Cohen • LGSW

Permanent Housing Case Manager

After graduating from McDaniel College with a Bachelor’s degree in Social Work, Lauren found her passion in the human services / non-profit field and went on to obtain a Master’s degree in Social Work with a Clinical/Mental Health focus from the University of Maryland, Baltimore School of Social Work. Lauren provides supportive services and case management to chronically homeless and disabled individuals in LARS’ Permanent Housing Program, and oversees LARS’ Transitional Housing Program.

Laura Wellford

Development & Marketing Manager

With deep family roots in the Laurel community, Laura is committed to ensuring that all of Laurel’s residents are given help when they need it and treated with the dignity they deserve. As Development & Marketing Manager, Laura seeks to engage the community with LARS’ mission in fun and meaningful ways. From writing grants to organizing large-scale fundraising events, Laura plays a key role in fueling LARS’ day to day operations.

Kennesha Rodgers

Bilingual Case Manager

Kennesha is a Prince George’s County native and received a B.A. in Psychology with a minor in Spanish language from the University of Maryland, College Park. She provides much needed support and outreach to LARS’ Spanish-speaking clients. Kennesha is passionate about advocacy and seeing underprivileged communities provided with opportunities for advancement. When she’s not in the Crisis Center at LARS, Kennesha loves traveling, spending time with her family, and participating in her local church community.

Angelica Christian

Case Manager & Volunteer Coordinator

Inspired at a youth development program in inner city DC, Angelica quickly developed a passion for helping low-income families and individuals. She earned a bachelor’s degree in psychology and family science from the University of Maryland, College Park. Raised by Puerto Rican parents and driven by the desire to master the Spanish language, she obtained a certificate in Spanish Studies from the University of Navarra in Pamplona, Spain. This supplements her solid educational foundation centered around understanding the individual, the family, and the environment in which they interact.

Janada Randall • LBSW

Transitional Housing Case Manager

Born and raised in Prince George’s County, Janada is passionate about serving her community as LARS’ Transitional Housing Case Manager and Employment Specialist. She received her Bachelors of Social Work degree with Magna Cum Laude honors from the illustrious Bennett College for Women. Janada has utilized her passion for social change by assisting low-income persons obtain knowledge on various life skills topics such as financial budgeting, parenting, health & wellness, and nutrition. Janada’s life motto: “When life throws you lemons, make sweet lemonade.”

Lynette Greenwood

Office Manager

Lynette’s infectious smile is the first thing people see when they walk through LARS’ door. She is the master multitasker who keeps our very busy office functioning like a well-oiled machine. Lynette joined LARS in 2009, bringing 30 years of secretarial experience to her role as Office Manager.
