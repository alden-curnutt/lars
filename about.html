Get To Know LARS

Laurel Advocacy and Referral Services, Inc. (LARS) is a registered 501(c)(3) non-profit organization serving homeless and low-income families and individuals in Laurel, Maryland, which includes parts of Howard County, Anne Arundel County, and Prince George’s County. LARS was founded in 1987 by local religious congregations, including churches and synagogues, for the purpose of assisting those experiencing homelessness and economic hardship. Although LARS does not proselytize or engage in religious activities, Laurel’s religious congregations remain key partners and vital sources of support for LARS.

Mission & Vision

Our Mission is to enable homeless and low-income people in Laurel who are in crisis to achieve stability and long-term self-sufficiency.

Our Vision is a community that treats all people with dignity and helps everyone meet their basic needs.

How did LARS, Inc. Get Started?

In 1987, a group of local Laurel pastors and laypeople came together to develop a consolidated approach to addressing the number of people who were coming to their doors seeking assistance.

Religious partner organizations help to support LARS financially and often provide volunteer assistance. Proselytizing is not allowed, although helping clients develop their spiritual resources has been encouraged as part of a holistic approach of LARS' services.

How Is LARS Funded?

LARS' funding comes from a variety of sources, including federal, state, local, and private grants, support from partnering congregations, community members, and fundraising.

Whatever it is that we do to help an individual or family in need, we do our best to convey that we are providing "crisis intervention." Our primary goal is to help families become more self-sufficient. We do not want to be a "band-aid" for crisis, but rather a bridge that helps families and individuals overcome barriers to self-sufficiency.
